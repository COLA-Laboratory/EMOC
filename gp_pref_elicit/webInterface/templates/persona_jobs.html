Experiment: Job offers

Supporting Multi-Objective Decision Making.

You need to work 2 days at home to take care of your new-born. You care about a good salary and job security.

The ordering of the importance of those issues is: Work from home > Salary > Probation time.

In this experiment, your goal is to find the best job offer, according to the persona description we give you.

Each job offer has the following attributes:
  • Work From Home: number of days you can work from home, e.g. "0.5" (half a day).
  • Salary: per month, before tax, in Euros, e.g. "1500".
  • Probation time: probation time in months, e.g. "2".

Persona Description

You describe yourself as follows:
“I have a seven-year old daughter and a new-born son. I don’t need to live in wealth, but to meet their expenses, it would be great if I can agree on a high salary and have some job security. I don't want to have a long probation time. Furthermore, since my partner is working on Mondays and Fridays, I need to take care of our new-born son on those days. Therefore I need to be able to work from home for two days in a week, but not necessarily more. If I have a very high salary I could also work from home less often, and pay a nanny instead.”

The ordering of the importance of those issues is:
Work from home > Salary > Probation time
(where issue1 > issue2 means that issue1 is more important than issue2 for you).

Examples:

Given these jobs:

Job1:
  • Work From Home: 3
  • Salary: 1500
  • Probation time: 3
Job2:
  • Work From Home: 2
  • Salary: 2000
  • Probation time: 6

you would prefer job2, because it has the higher salary and you can work two days from home. You don't care about working more days from home, and a high salary is more important than a short probation time.

If you are given these jobs:

Job1:
  • Work From Home: 2
  • Salary: 1500
  • Probation time: 6
Job2:
  • Work From Home: 1
  • Salary: 2000
  • Probation time: 3

you would prefer job1, because even though job2 has a very high salary and shorter probation time, you cannot work 2 days from home. If the salary was much higher in job offer 2 (for example 4000) then maybe this could convince you to work only one day from home.
